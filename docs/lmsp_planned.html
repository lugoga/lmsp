Bagamoyo Sustainable Fisheries & Tourism MSP

Bagamoyo District, Coast Region
Launch: Q3 2026 | Duration: 3 years
Community-based MSP integrating artisanal fisheries, seaweed farming, and cultural tourism. Will build on Kinondoni experience to expand sustainable livelihoods across the coast.
Expected Outcomes
  • 2,000 ha fishing zones designated with 200+ fishers participating
  • 500 ha seaweed farming areas established (300+ farmers)
  • Cultural tourism corridor developed (5-8 community sites)
  • $120,000/year livelihood support during transition
Funding & Partners
GEF-7 MSP Program, IUCN, Bagamoyo District Council. Estimated budget: $850,000
Artisanal Fisheries Seaweed Farming Cultural Tourism

Rufiji Delta & Mangrove Protection MSP

Chalinze District, Coast Region
Launch: Q4 2026 | Duration: 4 years
Comprehensive ecosystem-based MSP for major river delta ecosystem. Focus on mangrove restoration, fisheries management, and livelihood adaptation to climate change.
Key Components
  • 15,000 ha mangrove protection zone with community patrols
  • 3 seasonal fishing closure areas to protect breeding grounds
  • 500+ fisher transition program (aquaculture, mangrove restoration)
  • Climate adaptation: flood-resilient infrastructure for 5 villages
Implementation Timeline
2026: Stakeholder engagement & data collection
2027: Zoning finalization & livelihood programs
2028: Full implementation & monitoring systems
Mangrove Restoration Ecosystem-based Climate Adaptation

üåä Southern Tanzania (Kilwa & Songea)

üü° Planned

Kilwa Artisanal Fisheries & Islands MSP

Kilwa District, Lindi Region
Launch: Q2 2026 | Duration: 3 years
Unique island-based MSP managing artisanal fisheries in archipelago setting with historical and cultural significance. Will integrate heritage conservation with sustainable fisheries.
Distinguishing Features
  • 7 major islands with unique ecosystems & fishing traditions
  • UNESCO World Heritage site integration (Kilwa Kisiwani)
  • Traditional fishing methods preservation with sustainability
  • Marine archaeology protection in designated conservation zones
Expected Outputs
  • 3,500 ha fishing zones mapped by ecosystem
  • 1,200+ fishers in sustainable practice agreements
  • Heritage tourism: 2 archaeological sites with visitor facilities
  • Baseline: 280+ fishery-dependent households
Heritage Conservation Island-based MSP Archaeological Protection

Tanga Region-Wide MSP Coordination

All coastal districts in Tanga Region
Launch: Q1 2027 | Duration: 5 years
Regional-level coordination framework integrating existing district MSPs into coherent region strategy. Will address transboundary fisheries management and regional economies of scale.
Regional Integration Goals
  • Standardized zoning framework across 4 coastal districts
  • Joint monitoring system & surveillance network
  • Regional fisheries data hub & information system
  • Transboundary agreements with Kenya for shared stocks
Phasing Approach
Phase 1 (2027): Regional coordination framework & harmonization
Phase 2 (2027-2028): Joint systems implementation
Phase 3 (2028+): Scaling to other regions
Regional Coordination Transboundary Integration

üåä Blue Economy Integration

üü° Planned

Tanzanian Blue Economy MSP Integration

Multi-district (Dar, Tanga, Zanzibar, Mtwara)
Launch: Q3 2027 | Duration: Ongoing framework
Cross-cutting initiative integrating blue economy sectors (aquaculture, shipping, renewable energy, oil/gas) with MSP. Will create framework for sustainable ocean economy while protecting ecosystems.
Blue Economy Sectors
  • Aquaculture: 2,000+ ha designated for sustainable farming (fish & seaweed)
  • Shipping: Port development integrated with fisheries protection
  • Renewable Energy: Wave/tidal energy zones away from sensitive habitats
  • Oil & Gas: Production zones with strict environmental compliance
  • Tourism: 50+ sustainable ecotourism enterprises by 2028
Expected Economic Impact
$500M+ annual blue economy value with 15,000+ jobs created while maintaining ecological integrity
Blue Economy Multi-sector Economic Development
