Unguja Mangrove & Seaweed MSP (Pilot)

Unguja Island, Zanzibar
2015-2019 | 4 years
First formal MSP initiative in Tanzania. Pilot project demonstrating community-based planning integration with seaweed farming and mangrove conservation in island setting.
Project Achievements
Conservation: 2,850 ha mangrove forest protected | Fish catch increase 35% in reserve zones (2-year period)
Livelihood: 800 seaweed farmers organized in 4 cooperatives | $180,000 annual income from MSP zones
Governance: Steering Committee with 28 members (39% female) | 2,200 community members engaged
Key Lessons Learned
  • Community co-management model most effective for island settings
  • Seaweed farming integration increases livelihood security significantly
  • Strong government support critical for sustainability
  • Women's participation directly correlates with economic benefits
Pilot Project Mangrove Seaweed Success Model

Pemba Artisanal Fisheries Zoning (Pilot Phase 2)

Pemba Island, Zanzibar
2017-2021 | 4 years
Second pilot expanding MSP approach to larger island with diverse fishing fleet. Focus on artisanal and small-scale commercial fisheries coordination.
Implementation Results
Zoning System: 5 fishing zones designated | 1,200+ fishers registered | 15% increase in average catch per unit effort
Conflict Resolution: Fishing disputes reduced by 78% | Court cases drop from 12/year to 3/year
Youth Engagement: 120+ youth employed in fishing & aquaculture | 40 women in seaweed farming
Sustainability Mechanisms
  • User fee system: 2% of catch value supports management ($25,000/year revenue)
  • Fishing license integration with MSP zones (government mainstreamed)
  • Community patrol system: 20 trained rangers maintain compliance
  • Annual review & zone adjustment process established
Fisheries Zoning Conflict Resolution Sustainable Financing

Kunduchi Marine Reserve Planning & Establishment

Kunduchi, Dar es Salaam
2018-2023 | 5 years
First fully implemented and operational marine reserve in Dar. Demonstrates successful integration of reef protection, sustainable fishing, and community-controlled tourism revenue.
Completed Deliverables
Protected Area: 850 ha marine reserve with 3 management zones | Full demarcation & signage complete
Reef Health: Coral cover recovery from 12% to 31% | Fish biomass increased 280% (5-year baseline)
Economic Benefits: $95,000/year tourism revenue | 4 dive operators | 280+ jobs in tourism/hospitality
Livelihood Transition: 340 fishers transitioned | 180 in seaweed farming | 160 in tourism trades
Governance Handover
Full management responsibility transferred to Dar es Salaam City Council (2023). Operating independently with community co-management committee guiding decisions.
Marine Reserve Reef Restoration Tourism Integration Government Mainstreaming

Oysterbay & Msasani Coastal Development MSP

Oysterbay & Msasani, Dar es Salaam
2019-2024 | 5 years
Urban coastal MSP managing development pressure while maintaining fishing access and ecosystem services. First MSP in densely populated urban setting.
Key Outcomes Achieved
Development Control: 2 high-rise projects integrated with fisheries | 3 beach access paths preserved | 5 landing sites modernized
Fisheries Protection: 1,200 artisanal fishers maintained access | 4 coastal villages retained livelihoods
Environmental: 45 ha mangrove protection | Turtle nesting site restoration (250+ hatchlings/year)
Social Equity: Benefit-sharing from development: $120,000/year to community fund
Innovation: Urban MSP Model
Developed replicable framework for managing coastal development in cities. Now being adapted by other African cities (Mombasa, Maputo).
Urban MSP Development Control Innovation Model

‚úÖ Success Factors Across Completed Initiatives

Common Success Elements
  • Political Leadership: Government champion at district/city level committed to implementation
  • Community Ownership: Communities drive decisions; outsiders facilitate rather than direct
  • Phased Implementation: Realistic timelines (3-5 years); clear phase milestones
  • Livelihood Alternatives: Income support during transition critical for community buy-in
  • Financial Sustainability: User fees, revenue-sharing, or government budget integration
  • Adaptive Management: Regular review & adjustment based on monitoring data
  • Gender Inclusion: Women in leadership roles (committees >35% female)
Scaling & Replication
  • Unguja/Pemba pilots became foundation for Zanzibar-wide program
  • Kunduchi model replicated to 3 additional reef sites (Tanga, Zanzibar)
  • Urban MSP approach adapted by Dar, Mombasa, Maputo
  • District steering committees now standard in all new initiatives
  • Government budgets now allocate for MSP maintenance (previously all external funding)
Impact Metrics (10-Year Period)
  • 8,200+ households with improved livelihoods
  • $1.2M in annual economic benefits (fishing, tourism, aquaculture)
  • 18,000 ha marine ecosystems under management
  • 35% average increase in fish catch in protected areas
  • 280+ jobs created in tourism & alternative livelihoods
  • 78% reduction in resource use conflicts
