üîç Data Gaps & Additional Collection
Identified Data Gaps
  • Economic Data: Limited price series for some local products, informal sector valuation challenges
  • Biophysical Data: Incomplete historical ecosystem extent data, species population trends for some fauna
  • Social Data: Subsistence consumption values, cultural practice valuation complexities
  • Market Data: Informal trade value, non-market transaction documentation
  • Climate Data: High-resolution future projection scenarios, localized climate impacts
Additional Field Data Collection
  • Household willingness-to-pay surveys for ecosystem services
  • Market chain analysis and price tracking
  • Seasonal variation assessment for provisioning services
  • Expert elicitation for cultural and supporting service values
  • Climate scenario modeling and vulnerability assessment

üéØ Conclusion

The economic valuation of coastal and marine ecosystem goods and services in Mkuranga and Mkoani districts reveals substantial economic values dependent on ecosystem integrity. These findings demonstrate the critical economic case for ecosystem conservation and sustainable resource management. The baseline valuation, combined with risk analysis, provides compelling evidence for investment in conservation and sustainable development pathways.
