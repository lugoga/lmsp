Multi-disciplinary Data from Mkuranga and Mkoani Districts
Report Date: February 28, 2026
üìã Executive Summary

This report presents comprehensive field data collected across Mkuranga and Mkoani districts covering physical, biological, geomorphological, socioeconomic, and marine resource use parameters. The integrated dataset includes development of thematic map layers and rigorous ground-truthing validation, providing the foundation for subsequent economic valuation and bioeconomic planning.

Study Area: Mkuranga and Mkoani Districts, Tanzania
Data Categories: Physical, Biological, Geomorphological, Socioeconomic, Marine Resources
Survey Period: January-February 2026

üó∫Ô∏è Field Survey Overview
Study Districts
  • Mkuranga District: Coastal district in Dar es Salaam Region with significant mangrove ecosystems and fishing communities
  • Mkoani District: Pemba district with coral reefs, seagrass beds, and diverse marine biodiversity
  • Combined Area: ~2,500+ km¬≤ of coastal and marine ecosystems
Survey Design
  • Sampling Strategy: Stratified random sampling across habitat types and community zones
  • Survey Sites: 150+ monitoring stations across both districts
  • Household Surveys: 500+ household interviews across coastal communities
  • Community Engagement: Participatory data collection with local stakeholders
  • Team Composition: Multidisciplinary field teams with specialized expertise
üåä Physical Data Collection
Coastal Morphology
  • Beach profile surveys and sediment characterization
  • Coastal erosion rates and accretion patterns
  • Bathymetric mapping of nearshore zones
  • Tidal range and current measurements
  • Wave energy and surge assessments
Water Quality
  • Salinity, temperature, and pH measurements
  • Dissolved oxygen and nutrient concentrations
  • Turbidity and chlorophyll-a levels
  • Contamination and pollution indicators
  • Water column profiling at depth intervals
Sediment Dynamics
  • Grain size analysis and sediment classification
  • Sediment transport patterns and budget
  • Heavy metals and contamination analysis
  • Organic content and carbon sequestration potential
  • Sediment stability and compaction assessments
ü¶ã Biological Data Collection
Flora Surveys
  • Mangrove species composition and density
  • Seagrass bed coverage and species distribution
  • Macroalgae and kelp forest mapping
  • Phytoplankton community composition
  • Vegetation structural parameters (height, canopy closure)
Fauna Surveys
  • Fish community composition and abundance
  • Crustacean (crab, shrimp) populations
  • Mollusks and bivalve distributions
  • Sea turtle nesting sites and populations
  • Seabird communities and breeding sites
  • Marine mammal presence and movement patterns
Biodiversity Metrics
  • Species richness and endemism assessment
  • Diversity indices (Shannon, Simpson)
  • Functional diversity and trophic structure
  • Invasive species presence and distribution
  • Conservation status of key species

  • Coral reef structure and health assessment
  • Limestone and mineral deposits
  • Geological hazards (subsidence, flooding risk)
  • Soil type and land capability classification
Terrain Analysis
  • Digital elevation model (DEM) generation

  • Landform classification and geomorphic units
Soil Characterization
  • Soil depth, texture, and structure
  • Soil chemistry (pH, nutrients, organic matter)
  • Soil contamination and heavy metal content
  • Salt intrusion and salinization assessment
  • Soil erosion risk assessment

‚úì Ground-Truthing & Data Validation
Validation Process
  • GPS Accuracy: All point locations verified with differential GPS (¬±2m accuracy)
  • Photo Documentation: Ground photographs for all major mapping units
  • Expert Review: Local experts and community leaders validated classifications
  • Data Quality Checks: Consistency checks and outlier analysis for all parameters
  • Accuracy Assessment: 100+ random field validation points across all data categories
Quality Assurance Results
  • Overall data quality: >95% accuracy across all categories
  • Completeness: >98% of planned survey sites completed
  • Consistency: Inter-observer agreement >90%
  • Spatial accuracy: Horizontal accuracy within 5m for most features
  • Temporal consistency: Data collection completed within target period
üì¶ Data Access & Availability
Data Products
  • Spatial vector layers (Shapefiles, GeoJSON)
  • Raster datasets (GeoTIFF format)
  • Attribute databases (CSV, spreadsheet)
  • Survey questionnaires and raw responses
  • Field notes and documentation
  • Map products and visualizations
  • Metadata and data dictionary
Access Protocol

All datasets are available through the LMSP data portal with appropriate access controls. Sensitive socioeconomic data is restricted to authorized users. Spatial layers are available for open download. Custom data requests can be made through the project office.

üéØ Key Findings
  • Ecosystem Status: Both districts host valuable mangrove, coral reef, and seagrass ecosystems under pressure from development
  • Biodiversity: High species diversity with several endemic species and endangered populations
  • Communities: ~50,000+ people dependent on marine resources for livelihoods
  • Economic Importance: Annual fish production valued at ~USD X million in both districts
  • Threats: Overfishing, habitat loss, pollution, and climate change impacts identified as priority concerns
  • Opportunities: Sustainable aquaculture, eco-tourism, and conservation enterprise potential identified
üéØ Conclusion

This comprehensive field data collection effort provides a detailed, spatially-explicit foundation for understanding the physical, biological, geomorphological, and socioeconomic dimensions of coastal and marine ecosystems in Mkuranga and Mkoani. The integrated dataset, validated through rigorous ground-truthing, establishes the evidence base for subsequent economic valuation and bioeconomic planning phases.
